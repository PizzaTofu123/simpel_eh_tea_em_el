Development of Southeast Asian Countries

South East Asia or SEA for short is the geographical south-eastern region of Asia including countries such as Brunei, Cambodia, East Timor, Indonesia, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philipines, Singapore, Thailand and Vietnam. In this visualisation, I will be comparing the aforementioned countries based on social, political, macro economical and environmental data that define how well a country has developed throughout the years.

LIFE EXPECTANCY

One of the best indicators to seeing how well a country performs in general is to look at its Life expectancy which represents the average years or lifespan a person will have be expected to have if they were to live in the country. It can also be used to represent the quality of healthcare and the level of sanitation of the country.

From the presented map of the countries and their corresponding life expectancy throughout the years, it is clear that the life expectancy have been increasing for all the countries throughout decades of development. This can be caused by the economic improvement of the country itself or just by the improvement of life qualities of the entire world. Singapore has the highest Life expectancy of 83.44 years while Myanmar has the lowest Life expectancy of 65.672 years in the most recent year that the data has captured of year 2021.

POPULATION DENSITY & OVERALL POPULATION COUNT

Overall the population density of most of the Southeast Asian countries have been increasing steadily with a population density level higher than the World average, meaning that the Southeast Asian countries are more packed and dense than other countries in the world. Although most of the countries have pretty similar population density growth rate, Singapore has always had the highest population density and also is the fastest growing when compared to all the countries, while other countries number in the hundreds, Singapore is already in the thousands but has seen a plateau in growth in recent years.
