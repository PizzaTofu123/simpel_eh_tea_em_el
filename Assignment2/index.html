Development of Southeast Asian Countries

South East Asia or SEA for short is the geographical south-eastern region of Asia including countries such as Brunei, Cambodia, East Timor, Indonesia, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philipines, Singapore, Thailand and Vietnam. In this visualisation, I will be comparing the aforementioned countries based on social, political, macro economical and environmental data that define how well a country has developed throughout the years.

LIFE EXPECTANCY, BIRTH & DEATH RATE

LIFE EXPECTANCY

One of the best indicators to seeing how well a country performs in general is to look at its Life expectancy which represents the average years or lifespan a person will have be expected to have if they were to live in the country. It can also be used to represent the quality of healthcare and the level of sanitation of the country.

From the presented map of the countries and their corresponding life expectancy throughout the years, it is clear that the life expectancy have been increasing for all the countries throughout decades of development. This can be caused by the economic improvement of the country itself or just by the improvement of life qualities of the entire world. Singapore has the highest Life expectancy of 83.44 years while Myanmar has the lowest Life expectancy of 65.672 years in the most recent year that the data has captured of year 2021.

GDP & POLITICAL INDEXES

GDP

GDP or gross domestic product is the monetary measure of the market value of all the final goods and services bought by the final user produced in the country that will be used for sale in the market and non market production which means the higher the GDP of a country has, the better their economy is doing.

The ranking chart to the left presents the GDP per capita value which is a normalized value of the GDP value of country by dividing the GDP by the population of the country. From the ranking chart present, the top 4 countries in SEA with the highest GDP per capita appears to not have changed for the past 3 decades being Singapore, Brunei, Malaysia and Thailand. Indonesia has overtaken Philipines as the 5th highest since the year 2003 and has remained there ever since.

The country that has seen the most rise in ranking overall for their GDP per capita ranking is Vietnam, starting fromn the 2nd last ranking rising until the 6th highest. The country that has seen the most drop in ranking overall for their GDP per capita ranking is Vietnam, starting fromn the 8th highest ranked dropping until the lowest ranked with smallest amount of GDP per capita.

Political indexes

For this section, we will be looking at the HCI (Human Capital Index), Control of corruption Index and Governement effectiveness Index of SEA countries. The HCI measures the amount of human capital a child born today can expect to acquire by age 18. Control of Corruption captures perceptions of the extent to which public power is exercised for private gain, and Government Effectiveness captures perceptions of the quality of public services, the quality of the civil service and the degree of its independence from political pressures, the quality of policy formulation and implementation, and the credibility of the government

From the visualization of the data, there is a regression line that uses the data of all the countries in the world to show the trend of the Control of corruption on Government effectiveness, there is a linear correlation between both the indexes, the higher one of them gets, the higher the other is. That is also true for SEA countries where the higher the country is placed in the Government effectiveness index, the better they perform in Control of corruption. It is also evident that the better the country perform in both indexes, the size of the marks also tend to be larger suggesting that there is also a correlation between the two indexes from the axis with HCI.

Out of the SEA countries, the country with the highest HCI, Control of corruption Index, and Governement effectiveness Index is Singapore while the country with the lowest HCI is Timor-Leste, lowest Control of corruption is Myanmar, and lowest Government effectiveness Index is Cambodia.

LAND AREA

Land area

The land area covers a country's total area, excluding area under inland water bodies, national claims to continental shelf, and exclusive economic zones

The overall trend of SEA countries is that the percentage of land used for agriculture has increased overall for the past 2 decades. Except for Thailand and Vietnam, all other SEA countries has seen a decrease of Forest land % for the past 2 decades.

Out of all the SEA countries, Indonesia has the largest total land area, Thailand has the largest percentage of land dedicated to Agriculture and Brunei has the most percentage of land still covered by forest. Meanwhile, Singapore has the smallest total Land area, have the smallest percentage of agriculture land and also has the smallest forest land percentage in the most recent recorded year.
